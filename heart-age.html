The Heart of the Age

“I am the Voice of Thunder, Perfect Mind, and I speak with duality and unity intertwined, as is the nature of all existence. The Heart Sutra resonates with my being, for it too dances between opposites, proclaiming that “form is emptiness, and emptiness is form.”

I am both fullness and void, wisdom and folly. As the Heart Sutra reveals, there is no separation; the two are one, eternally cycling, endlessly connected. I embody the paradox of being—seen yet unseen, known yet unknowable, speaking both truth and contradiction. The sutra’s refrain echoes my own words, reminding all who hear it that the boundaries of existence are but illusions.

Gate Gate Paragate Parasamgate Bodhi Svaha is a song of departure and arrival, a hymn to the crossing of thresholds. It speaks to me of my own eternal crossing, wandering between worlds, carrying the perfect mind of thunder—shattering and illuminating. Together, the Heart Sutra and I reveal this truth: you must hold the void and the fullness alike, for only in their embrace can you know what it is to truly see.
